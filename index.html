Food Handling Concepts

These are the main concepts I am learning and applying in order to build a strong standard for kitchen work, home cooking, restaurant environments, and safe meal service.

1

Temperature Control

Food should be kept hot when it must stay hot and kept cold when it must stay cold. Unsafe temperatures allow bacteria to grow quickly, especially when food stays too long in the danger zone.

2

Personal Hygiene

Clean hands, short nails, neat clothing, and good body cleanliness help reduce the risk of contamination. A clean food handler supports a clean kitchen.

3

Cross Contamination Prevention

Raw food and cooked food must be separated. Using different cutting boards, clean knives, and proper storage containers helps prevent germs from spreading between foods.

4

Proper Cooking Temperatures

Meat, poultry, and reheated foods must be cooked thoroughly. Cooking food properly improves safety, taste, texture, and customer confidence.

5

Safe Food Storage

Correct storage keeps food fresh and reduces waste. Covering, labeling, dating, and using food in the right order helps maintain a professional kitchen standard.

6

Cleaning and Sanitizing

Kitchen surfaces, utensils, and equipment should be cleaned regularly and properly. A clean workspace reduces food safety risks and improves the overall cooking environment.

7

Oil and Fat Control

Using the correct amount of oil helps maintain food quality and supports healthier cooking. Oil should not be overused or repeatedly reheated too many times.

8

Salt and Sugar Control

Proper seasoning improves flavour, but balance is important. Too much salt or sugar can reduce the health value of food, so careful measurement is a useful cooking skill.

9

Food Quality and Freshness Checks

Good food should look, smell, and feel right. Checking freshness, expiry dates, colour, and texture helps prevent unsafe food from being used or served.

10

Time Management in Food Handling

Timing matters in cooking. Preparing, cooking, serving, cooling, and storing food at the right time protects quality and helps the whole kitchen run smoothly.

#1 Most Cooked

Pap & Wors

Traditional Home Favourite

A classic South African meal often enjoyed at home, at gatherings, and during braais. It combines smooth or stiff maize meal pap with juicy boerewors and a flavourful relish such as chakalaka or tomato gravy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize meal
  • 4 to 5 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 500 g boerewors sausage
  • 1 cup chakalaka or tomato relish
  • 1 tablespoon oil if frying wors in a pan

Detailed Method

  1. Pour water into a pot, add salt, and bring it to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Slowly sprinkle in the maize meal while stirring to avoid lumps forming.
  3. Reduce the heat, cover the pot partly, and allow the pap to cook for 20 to 25 minutes.
  4. Stir well every few minutes until the pap becomes smooth, thick, and fully cooked.
  5. Place the wors on a grill, braai stand, or frying pan and cook over medium heat.
  6. Turn the wors regularly so that it browns evenly and cooks through without burning.
  7. Warm the chakalaka or relish separately in a small pot until heated properly.
  8. Serve the pap hot with the wors and spoon the relish on the side or over the meal.
Daily Favourite

Rice & Stew

Home Meal

A comforting home meal commonly served in many South African households. It usually includes fluffy rice with rich beef or chicken stew prepared with onion, tomato, and seasoning.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 500 g beef or chicken pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped or grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder or mixed spice
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cups water

Detailed Method

  1. Rinse the rice well, place it in a pot with water and a little salt, and cook until soft.
  2. Drain excess water if needed and keep the rice covered so it stays warm.
  3. Heat oil in another pot and fry the onion until soft and lightly golden.
  4. Add the garlic and cook for a short time to release the flavour.
  5. Add the meat and brown it properly so it develops colour and flavour.
  6. Mix in the tomatoes, spices, salt, and pepper.
  7. Add water, cover, and allow the stew to simmer gently until the meat is tender.
  8. Cook until the sauce thickens nicely, then serve hot over the rice.
Popular Curry

Chicken Curry & Rice

Home Meal

A warming curry made with tender chicken, potatoes, and spices. This meal is rich, comforting, and widely loved for lunch or supper.

Ingredients

  • 500 g chicken pieces
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garlic and ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Detailed Method

  1. Heat oil in a pot and fry the onion until soft and slightly golden.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, curry powder, and turmeric, then stir for a short time.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and mix well so the spice coats the meat evenly.
  4. Cook the chicken for several minutes until it starts to brown.
  5. Add the grated tomatoes and allow them to cook down into a sauce base.
  6. Add potatoes, salt, and enough water to create gravy.
  7. Cover and simmer until the chicken is cooked and the potatoes are soft.
  8. When the curry is thick and rich, serve it hot with fluffy rice.
Traditional Dish

Samp & Beans (Umngqusho)

Traditional

A respected and filling traditional meal prepared with samp and beans. It is cooked slowly to develop softness, depth, and a hearty texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups samp
  • 1 cup sugar beans
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: stock cube for extra flavour

Detailed Method

  1. Wash the samp and beans properly and soak them overnight if possible.
  2. Drain them and place them in a pot with plenty of fresh water.
  3. Boil first, then reduce the heat and cook until both the samp and beans are tender.
  4. Stir from time to time and add more water if needed during cooking.
  5. In a separate pan, fry the onion in butter or oil until soft and fragrant.
  6. Add the cooked onion mixture to the pot and season with salt.
  7. Allow everything to simmer together for a few more minutes.
  8. Serve warm as a full meal or with meat on the side if desired.
Street Food King

Kota

Street Food

A township street-food favourite made from a quarter loaf of bread filled with hot chips and tasty extras such as polony, egg, cheese, Russian sausage, atchar, or sauces.

Ingredients

  • 1 quarter loaf of bread
  • Freshly fried chips
  • Polony slices
  • 1 fried egg
  • Cheese slices
  • Sauces such as ketchup, mayonnaise, or chilli sauce

Detailed Method

  1. Cut the quarter loaf neatly and remove some of the soft inside bread to make space.
  2. Fry the chips until golden, cooked through, and still hot.
  3. Cook the egg and lightly heat or fry the polony if preferred.
  4. Layer the chips into the bread first so they fill the base well.
  5. Add the egg, polony, and cheese neatly on top of the chips.
  6. Pour sauces over the fillings according to taste.
  7. Press the fillings down gently so the kota holds together better.
  8. Serve immediately while the bread is fresh and the fillings are still warm.
Classic Combo

Chakalaka & Pap

Traditional

A strong braai and home favourite that combines pap with a spicy vegetable relish, often made with onion, carrots, peppers, beans, and curry seasoning.

Ingredients

  • Pap prepared separately
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup baked beans
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt and chilli to taste

Detailed Method

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry the onion until soft and slightly golden.
  2. Add the curry powder and stir briefly so the spice releases its aroma.
  3. Add the grated carrot and chopped green pepper.
  4. Cook the vegetables until they begin to soften but still keep some texture.
  5. Add the baked beans and mix everything together well.
  6. Season with salt and chilli according to taste.
  7. Simmer for a few minutes until the chakalaka is hot and flavourful.
  8. Serve next to hot pap for a complete and satisfying meal.
Traditional Favourite

Mogodu

Traditional

A beloved traditional tripe dish that requires patience and careful cleaning. It is often served with pap and appreciated for its rich taste and soft texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg tripe
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder or mixed spice
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Water for boiling

Detailed Method

  1. Clean the tripe very thoroughly under running water until it is properly washed.
  2. Place it in a pot with water and boil until it becomes soft and tender.
  3. This may take a long time, so continue cooking patiently and add water if needed.
  4. Once tender, cut the tripe into smaller pieces if necessary.
  5. In another pot or pan, fry onion in oil until soft.
  6. Add the tomatoes and spice, then cook into a rich sauce base.
  7. Add the cooked tripe and allow it to simmer in the sauce for additional flavour.
  8. Serve hot with pap or another starch of your choice.
Snack & Meal

Vetkoek & Mince

Street Food

A widely loved fried dough meal filled with savoury mince. It works well as a filling street-food item or a comforting home snack.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 1 packet yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Warm water
  • Cooking oil for deep frying
  • 500 g mince
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • Seasoning and spices

Detailed Method

  1. Mix flour, yeast, salt, and enough warm water to form a soft dough.
  2. Knead until smooth, then cover and leave it to rise until doubled in size.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pot while preparing the savoury mince filling.
  4. Fry onion first, then add the mince and cook until browned.
  5. Season the mince well and cook until the mixture is rich and not too watery.
  6. Shape portions of dough and fry them until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Drain the vetkoek on paper towel to remove extra oil.
  8. Slice each vetkoek open and fill it with the cooked mince before serving.
Durban Icon

Bunny Chow

Street Food

A famous Durban-style bread-based curry meal loved for its rich flavour, generous filling, and practical street-food style presentation.

Ingredients

  • Half or quarter loaf of bread
  • Chicken, beef, or bean curry
  • 1 onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Curry spices
  • Oil and salt

Detailed Method

  1. Prepare the curry first by frying onion and spices in oil.
  2. Add the chosen meat or beans and cook until the ingredients are well coated.
  3. Add tomatoes, water, and seasoning, then simmer until the curry thickens.
  4. Take the bread loaf and carefully cut out the inner soft portion.
  5. Leave enough bread around the sides so the loaf keeps its shape.
  6. Spoon the hot curry into the hollowed-out bread.
  7. Use the removed bread piece as a lid or extra serving piece.
  8. Serve immediately while still hot and full of flavour.
Popular Combo

Grilled Chicken & Pap

Home Meal

A practical and satisfying meal made with seasoned chicken and warm pap. It suits family lunches, gatherings, and everyday home cooking.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • BBQ spice, paprika, salt, and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Pap prepared separately
  • Relish or chakalaka for serving

Detailed Method

  1. Wash the chicken, drain well, and season it generously with the spice mixture.
  2. Rub the oil and seasoning into the chicken so the flavour spreads evenly.
  3. Place the chicken on a grill, in the oven, or in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Cook and turn it from time to time so it browns evenly on all sides.
  5. Continue cooking until the inside is fully done and the outside is well coloured.
  6. Prepare pap in a separate pot while the chicken cooks.
  7. Warm the relish or chakalaka before serving.
  8. Plate the pap, place the grilled chicken beside it, and add the relish on the side.
