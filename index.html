Food Handling Concepts

These are the key concepts I am learning and applying to build a strong standard for food service work, restaurant environments, and home cooking.

1

Temperature Control

Keep hot food hot and cold food cold. The danger zone is where bacteria grow quickly, so food should not stay at unsafe temperatures for long periods.

2

Personal Hygiene

Clean hands, clean clothing, and neat food preparation habits reduce contamination and help protect customers and family members.

3

Cross Contamination Prevention

Raw and cooked foods should be kept separate. Utensils, chopping boards, and storage areas must be managed carefully.

4

Proper Cooking Temperatures

Food must be cooked thoroughly and reheated properly to make it safe, enjoyable, and suitable for service.

5

Safe Food Storage

Correct storage keeps food fresh, prevents waste, and improves kitchen organization through dating, covering, and first-in-first-out use.

6

Cleaning and Sanitizing

Surfaces, tools, and equipment must be cleaned regularly to maintain a professional kitchen standard and reduce food safety risks.

7

Oil and Fat Control

Using the right amount of oil and avoiding repeated reuse helps protect food quality, taste, and overall health value.

8

Salt and Sugar Control

Balanced seasoning improves taste while helping keep meals healthier and more suitable for regular consumption.

9

Food Quality and Freshness Checks

Checking smell, appearance, texture, and freshness helps maintain kitchen standards and protect the people being served.

10

Time Management in Food Handling

Preparing, serving, and storing food at the right time helps preserve taste, texture, and safety throughout the kitchen workflow.

South African / African Meals

#1 Most Cooked

Pap & Wors

Traditional Home Favourite

A South African staple often enjoyed at home and during braais, served with rich chakalaka or relish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize meal
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt
  • Boerewors sausage
  • Chakalaka or tomato relish

Method

  1. Boil salted water and slowly add maize meal.
  2. Stir until thick and cook through.
  3. Grill or fry the wors until fully cooked.
  4. Serve with chakalaka or relish.
Daily Favourite

Rice & Stew

Home Meal

A comforting meal served in many homes with beef or chicken cooked in a rich tomato based stew.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Beef or chicken pieces
  • Onion, tomato, garlic
  • Curry powder or mixed spices

Method

  1. Cook rice separately.
  2. Fry onion and garlic, then brown the meat.
  3. Add tomato and seasoning.
  4. Simmer until tender and serve over rice.
Popular Curry

Chicken Curry & Rice

Home Meal

A rich, warming curry with tender chicken and potatoes, often served with fluffy rice.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • Curry powder
  • Potatoes
  • Onion and tomato

Method

  1. Fry onion with curry spices.
  2. Add chicken and brown lightly.
  3. Add potatoes, tomato, and water.
  4. Simmer until thick and serve with rice.
Traditional Dish

Samp & Beans (Umngqusho)

Traditional

A filling and respected traditional meal made with samp and beans, often prepared slowly for full flavour.

Ingredients

  • Samp
  • Sugar beans
  • Onion
  • Butter or oil

Method

  1. Soak samp and beans overnight if possible.
  2. Boil until soft and tender.
  3. Fry onion separately.
  4. Mix together, season, and serve warm.
Street Food King

Kota

Street Food

A popular township favourite made from quarter bread filled with hot chips and tasty toppings.

Ingredients

  • Quarter loaf of bread
  • Chips or fries
  • Polony, egg, cheese
  • Sauces of choice

Method

  1. Hollow out the bread slightly.
  2. Add hot chips.
  3. Add fillings and sauces.
  4. Serve immediately while hot.
Classic Combo

Chakalaka & Pap

Traditional

A strong braai and home classic that combines pap with a spicy vegetable and bean relish.

Ingredients

  • Beans
  • Carrots, onion, peppers
  • Curry powder and spices
  • Pap prepared separately

Method

  1. Fry onion and spices.
  2. Add vegetables and cook until tender.
  3. Add beans and simmer.
  4. Serve with hot pap.
Traditional Favourite

Mogodu

Traditional

A beloved traditional tripe dish prepared patiently for tenderness and served with pap or another starch.

Ingredients

  • Tripe
  • Onion and tomato
  • Curry powder or spices
  • Salt and seasoning

Method

  1. Clean the tripe thoroughly.
  2. Boil until soft and tender.
  3. Fry onion and tomato with spices.
  4. Add tripe and simmer before serving.
Snack & Meal

Vetkoek & Mince

Street Food

A widely loved fried dough meal filled with savoury mince, suitable for both home and street food style service.

Ingredients

  • Flour and yeast
  • Water and salt
  • Mince
  • Onion and seasoning

Method

  1. Prepare dough and allow it to rise.
  2. Fry until golden brown.
  3. Cook mince with onion and spice.
  4. Slice and fill the vetkoek before serving.
Durban Icon

Bunny Chow

Street Food

A famous bread based curry meal loved for its rich flavour and practical, filling presentation.

Ingredients

  • Half or quarter loaf of bread
  • Chicken, beef, or bean curry
  • Onion, tomato, spices

Method

  1. Prepare and cook the curry.
  2. Hollow out the bread carefully.
  3. Fill the bread with hot curry.
  4. Serve immediately.
Popular Combo

Grilled Chicken & Pap

Home Meal

A practical and satisfying meal built around seasoned chicken and warm pap, suitable for family meals and events.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • BBQ spice or seasoning
  • Pap
  • Relish or chakalaka

Method

  1. Season the chicken well.
  2. Grill, fry, or bake until cooked through.
  3. Prepare pap separately.
  4. Serve together with sauce or relish.
